About

The term "drone" dates back to the early days of aviation, when it was applied to remotely-flown target aircraft like the 1920s Fairey Queen and 1930s de Havilland Queen Bee that were used to train firing battleship guns. The Airspeed Queen Wasp and Miles Queen Martinet were later models, before being eventually replaced by the GAF Jindivik. The word is still widely used.

A powered, aerial vehicle that does not have a human operator, employs aerodynamic forces to create vehicle lift, may fly autonomously or be piloted remotely, can be expendable or recoverable, and can carry a lethal or nonlethal payload is characterized as an unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV). The phrase "unmanned aerial vehicle" (UAV) is widely used to refer to military applications. [14] However, warhead-carrying missiles are prohibited.
